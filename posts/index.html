10-24-2021

Since I am writing these at 1 am, each post technically corresponds to the day before.
On Saturdays without quizbowl, I always attend Go club meetings. I can tell the same story I tell everyone there - I learned to play weiqi when I was little and lived in China, did not play it for many years, but am now experiencing a "renaissance." At a cosmopolitan community such as Brown, I expected clubs of people that shared niche interests of mine, including weiqi. But I did not expect the combination of characters, some familiar and others new, both in their similarities to (many of us have study CS/math) and differences from (some were international students from China; others American) me. I want to catch up on how the game has changed in the last decade or so, especially with the advent of AI. I even watch Nick Sibicky now like I would chess channels.
Will there always be a moral to the day's story? Probably not.
